Future Self Guidance

Phase 1: Problem Space Exploration

Embrace the Non-Linear Journey
×

Your first instinct will be to create a linear path from problem to solution. Resist this urge. Innovation is messy, and you'll encounter 'stuck moments', frustrations, failed ideas and more before breakthrough insights emerge. Accept that your journey will be far from linear and prepare mentally for the emotional rollercoaster ahead.

Key Processes/Approaches/Methods:

  • Actant Mapping: Start here to identify all stakeholders, both human and non-human elements in your system. Don't limit yourself to obvious players – the most impactful solutions often address overlooked actants.
  • System Mapping and Actor Constellations: Use these methods to understand relationships and power dynamics between stakeholders. Identify who is most affected, currently, as well as secondary.
  • Miro Board Setup and Organisation: Set up a digital workspace as your "central hub" from day one. Allocate sections for each week, incorporate weekly challenges, and maintain a running to-do list.

Principle: Centre Your Stakeholders

Place your key stakeholders at the centre of your intervention from the beginning. In the AURA project, focusing on TSG employees, TSG clients, human agency, and attitudes toward AI ensured the solution remained "effective and highly targeted."

Phase 2: Deep Research and Insight Generation

Balance Primary and Secondary Research Strategically
×

Don't underestimate the power of conversations with real people. Primary research through interviews across various industries, generations, and organisational levels will reveal insights that desk alone research cannot. However, complement this with extensive secondary research to understand the broader landscape.

Key Processes/Approaches/Methods:

  • Theme Clustering and Affinity Mapping: After conducting interviews, use these methods to identify recurring themes. This is where breakthrough insights emerge.
  • Ball in the Basin Method: Use this to understand system dynamics and identify leverage points. Our realisation that the solution required a "mindset shift" rather than just information delivery came from this method's focus on balancing and reinforcing feedback loops.

Principle: Let Users Guide Your Innovation

Your interviews will reveal not just what studies or reports say about the problem space, but actually what the users themselves feel they need. We discovered that people had basic AI understanding but were held back by fears about accuracy and privacy.

Phase 3: Ideation and Concept Development

Leverage Team Diversity During Stuck Moments
×

When creativity stagnates, lean into your team's diverse perspectives. Use rapid ideation methods to break through mental blocks and generate unexpected connections. The concept of AURA emerged from quote-based ideation when we felt stuck, proving that breakthrough ideas often come when you least expect them.

Key Processes/Approaches/Methods:

  • Crazy 8s Ideation and Dotmocracy: Apply these during "stuck moments" to boost individual productivity and harness collective creativity. Use Dotmocracy to democratically converge on ideas that best target your core questions.
  • Quote-Based Ideation: This method, recommended by our mentor, was transformational to say the least. Select powerful quotes from interviews that capture the essence of user needs, then rapidly ideate solutions around them.
  • "Yes, And..." Method: As you approach prototyping, use this method to question potential pitfalls and opportunities constructively and optimistically.

Principle: Embrace Blue-Sky Thinking with Grounded Evaluation

Allow for unrestricted creativity during ideation but always filter ideas through your stakeholder lens, core insights and realistic grounding. The three concepts we identified emerged from creative sessions but were validated against user needs and stakeholder requirements.

Phase 4: Prototyping and Development

Choose Your Medium Strategically
×

Always consider how your solution will be integrated within your industry partner's existing ecosystem and their clients' workflows. Start with accessible, lower-level prototyping platforms that allow rapid iteration but be prepared to transition to more sophisticated tools if user experience and testing demands it.

Key Processes/Approaches/Methods:

  • Rapid Prototyping with Core Elements: Identify the minimum viable elements that communicate your value proposition effectively. Focus on 3-5 key components that demonstrate core functionality rather than attempting comprehensive feature development.
  • "Fail Fast" Mentality: Embrace extensive prototyping specifically to test assumptions and uncover limitations before significant resource investment. Create multiple versions quickly to identify potential issues like over-complexity, user fatigue, or accessibility barriers.

Principle: Prototype for Learning, Not Perfection

Your prototype should answer specific questions about feasibility and desirability, not showcase final polish. Focus on testing core assumptions and gathering feedback on fundamental concepts rather than perfecting aesthetics.

Phase 5: Testing and Feedback Integration

Test with Minimal Guidance
×

When testing your prototype, resist the urge to over-explain your solution. We offered AURA to 12 prospective users with minimal guidance about its purpose, which revealed genuine user perceptions and usability issues we might have missed with guided testing.

Key Processes/Approaches/Methods:

  • Structured User Testing: Create systematic feedback collection that covers both usability and content perception. Ask open-ended questions about purpose and value before diving into specific functionality feedback.
  • Navigation and Accessibility Assessment: Pay special attention to user journey issues. Our feedback revealed navigation problems that were invisible to us as creators but critical barriers for users.

Principle: Act on Constructive Feedback Immediately

Don't just collect feedback - implement changes quickly. We added page prompts, menu bars, and FAQ sections directly in response to user feedback. This responsiveness demonstrates commitment to user-centred design and improves your solution's market readiness.

Extra Tips & Collaboration Strategies

Managing Transdisciplinary Innovation
×

Managing Transdisciplinary Innovation with Industry Partners

Don't treat your industry partner as just a client - view them as a collaborator whose values and goals should influence your innovation process. Our continued commitment to aligning AURA with TSG's values and AI policy offerings ensured our solution enhanced rather than competed with their existing services.

Key Collaboration Strategies:

  • Regular Stakeholder Check-ins: Work directly with your industry partner to identify and validate key actants throughout the process.
  • Value Proposition Alignment: Ensure your solution supports your partner's broader business strategy. AURA was designed to enhance TSG's AI policy offering and support their commitment to an AI-ready workforce.
  • Sustainability Planning: Consider economic, social, environmental, and ethical factors from the beginning. Discuss implementation models early to ensure viability for all stakeholders.

Managing Team Dynamics and Persistence

Acknowledge that "feelings of creative block or mental drain" are normal, especially after long days or when dealing with conflicting schedules. These challenges aren't roadblocks - they're opportunities to strengthen collaboration and develop more realistic, user-centred solutions.

Key Takeaways for Future Collaboration:

  • Shared Commitment Over Individual Brilliance: Success comes from team persistence and willingness to keep iterating, even when things don't go to plan.
  • Communication as Foundation: Stay committed and communicative during difficult periods. Our most challenging moments forced better collaboration.
  • Iteration as Strategy: Embrace continuous refinement rather than seeking perfect initial solutions. AURA became more thoughtful and realistic through persistent iteration.

Core Principles for Future Self

Fundamental Guidelines for Innovation Success
×
  1. Trust the Process: Transdisciplinary innovation requires patience with ambiguity and comfort with non-linear progress. The breakthrough insights and meaningful connections often emerge from what initially feels like chaos.
  2. Centre the Human Experience: Every decision should be filtered through genuine user needs and stakeholder value. Technical feasibility and business viability matter, but human desirability should drive your innovation direction.
  3. Embrace Collaborative Vulnerability: Be willing to share incomplete ideas, admit when you're stuck, and ask for help. The best solutions emerge from collective intelligence, not individual expertise.
  4. Plan for Sustainability: Consider long-term viability across economic, social, environmental, and ethical dimensions from the beginning. Short-term solutions that create long-term problems serve no one.
  5. Measure Success by Impact, Not Output: The value of your innovation lies not in how clever or complex it is, but in how effectively it creates positive change for your stakeholders and addresses the original challenge.
